Coding for Teachers




This page will serve as a list of resources that teachers can use to promote the use of technology in the classroom.

1) Teach your students how to type

I've found that it's not wise to assume your students have been taught how to type. Use the following free resources to get your students typing quickly:

Typing Club 10fastfingers.com Google Docs

2) Teach your students how to code

The links below are great for all students, whether they have zero coding knowledge or have been coding for years:

scratch.mit.edu codepen.io freeCodeCamp.org w3schools.com code.org

3) Create awesome computer art

I found that many of my students were very interested in using computers to create art, so I started an after-school program called Blender Club. The following software is industry-level and available completely free, so it is perfect for students wishing to become animators, game developers, or graphic designers:

blender.org inkscape.org gimp.org

4) Refurbish old computers

After speaking to my schools' IT manager, I found that there were tons of old computers sitting around, waiting to be trashed. The IT manager gave me as many computers and computer parts that I wanted, so I had my students take them apart and replace any parts that weren't working. My students were amazed at how easy it was to fix computers, so I found it to be a great learning experience. We installed various versions of Linux on various machines and created our own computer lab in the back of the classroom. The versions of Linux, also known as distros, are freely available and are a great free alternative to other paid operating systems:

Linux Mint Ubuntu NOOBS

5) Game Development

If your school has access to high-end computers, there are some great, free programs that allow your students to develop their own apps and games:

Maya Unreal Engine 4 Unity Android Studio Corona

About




My name is Tallen Rand and I'm a huge computer nerd. I started teaching my 5th-grade students how to type and code after our school district purhased Chromebooks for everyone and tasked the teachers with integrating technology into the classroom. Blender is my single-most favorite piece of software, but I also enjoy using the Adobe Creative Suite and Unreal Engine 4. Programming, building computers, and playing video games are my main hobbies. I believe that all children should be computer-literate.

Currently, I am writing a Udemy course that details everything I did to teach my students how to code and create art with computers. Once my course is complete I will share a link to it on this page.
